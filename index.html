Portfolio

Featured Project: Point of Sale System App

Motivation: We (Rafael Ramirez & I) decided we wanted to improve POS systems in order to increase order accuracy and decrease ordering time for restaurant employees.

Online Study: We started by auditing 10 popular POS applications, along with their reviews. We found that many felt dated or clunky, and lacked customization options. Furthermore, we noticed they all seemed to follow the same general layout: all the menu items have their own button on the screen, and sub-items (i.e. burger meal or cheeseburger meal) had their own sub-screens. This led to a somewhat complicated page hierarchy, which our group believed was problem, and we wanted to address it.

Prototyping Phase: We created a few prototypes: starting with low-fidelity prototypes, we created two versions of what the app might look like in MS Paint, one utilizing radial menus for some items, and another following the same general layout with a button for each item. Following user study 1, we decided to proceed with the radial menu version. After another user study, we found that the standard layout actually performed better.

User Studies Results: User study 1 showed us that users enjoyed the experience of the radial menus in place of standard buttons, and that it made the system more intuitive. However, in our second user study, we found that while the radial menus felt better to the users, they actually hindered the speed of the user, with a negligible change in the accuracy of their orders. With this in mind, we decided to proceed to our final prototype without the radial menus in place.
